Paneer Kali Mirch

Tangy, peppery dry paneer with amchur. Completely different from every other paneer dish in the rotation.

Servings
5m
Prep
15m
Cook
20m
Total
300
kcal
Method
  1. 1 Heat ghee on medium-high. Sear paneer cubes on 2 sides until light golden (~2 min each). Remove
  2. 2 Same pan: add cumin seeds. Once they splutter add onion and green chilli. Cook 3 min
  3. 3 Add ginger garlic paste. Stir 1 min. Add tomato. Cook 2 min until broken down
  4. 4 Add turmeric, black pepper, salt. Stir 30 seconds
  5. 5 Add paneer back in. Add amchur. Toss on high heat 1 min. Finish with coriander
  6. 6 Stir-fry side vegetables separately with salt 3 min

♨️ Reheating: High heat pan 2 min. Add tiny splash of water if sticking.

Note: Amchur is the key ingredient — sharp tanginess without liquid. Find it at any provision store. Lemon as substitute works but is not the same.

Ingredients (1 serving)

Onion (finely sliced) 50g
Tomato (finely chopped) 50g
Green chilli 1
Ginger garlic paste ½ tsp
Black pepper (coarsely ground) ½ tsp
Cumin seeds ½ tsp
Turmeric ¼ tsp
Dry mango powder (amchur) ½ tsp
Fresh coriander 5g
Ghee 1 tsp
Spinach or beans (side) 60g
